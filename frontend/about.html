Project Vision

The AI Powered Fake News Detection Application is an intelligent platform designed to help users verify online information and protect themselves from misinformation. With the rapid spread of fake news across digital platforms, this project provides a reliable and easy-to-use solution for checking the authenticity of online content.

This model is designed to act as a classification signal within an evidence-based fact-checking system rather than a standalone decision-maker.

The FactCheck Engine

How our hybrid reasoning system works

Unlike simple classifiers that only look at text patterns, our system uses a multi-step reasoning pipeline (`factcheck_engine.py`) to verify claims against live evidence from the web.

1. Operator-Driven Analysis

The engine first identifies the intent of the claim (e.g., "Exclusivity" claims like 'First country to...', "Temporal" events, or "Set Membership"). This prevents generic processing.

2. Live Evidence Retrieval

It queries the live web (DuckDuckGo) to fetch the latest news snippets, ensuring the model isn't limited to its training data cutoff.

3. NLI Verification

We use a Natural Language Inference (NLI) model (`roberta-large-mnli`) to explicitly check for contradictions between the claim and the retrieved evidence.

4. Safety & Precision

If no sufficient evidence is found, the system defaults to "INSUFFICIENT" rather than hallucinating a verdict. This significantly reduces the limitations of standard LLMs.

Model Description

  • Architecture: DistilBERT (encoder-only Transformer)
  • Task: Binary text classification (REAL vs FAKE)
  • Labels: 0 → REAL / SUPPORTED, 1 → FAKE / MISINFORMATION
  • Input: Short factual claims, headlines, or summarized news text

Framework Versions

  • Transformers: 4.57.3
  • PyTorch: 2.9.0+cu126
  • Datasets: 4.0.0
  • Tokenizers: 0.22.1

Intended Uses

Primary: Acting as a classifier signal in an automated fake-news / fact-checking system; Assisting evidence-based verification pipelines.

Secondary: Academic research on misinformation detection; Educational demonstrations.

Limitations

The model may be overconfident on time-sensitive or emerging claims. It does not perform evidence retrieval or reasoning. Predictions should not be treated as final truth judgments. Performance may degrade on domains significantly different from training data.

Training Data & Procedure

The model was trained on a unified and normalized dataset shravan1nala/tejas-fake-news-dataset-v3 constructed from:

LIAR-2 Dataset

Short political claims; Multi-class labels normalized to binary; Human-annotated.

FakeNewsNet

News headlines and social-style misinformation; Binary real/fake labels.

ErfanMoosavi Dataset

Longer news articles; News-oriented misinformation patterns.

Training Hyperparameters

Learning Rate: 2e-5
Batch Size: 16
Epochs: 3 (Early Stopped)
Optimizer: AdamW (fused)
Weight Decay: 0.01
